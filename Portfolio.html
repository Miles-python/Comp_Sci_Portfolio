Python Turtle Art

Description:
In this project, we selected a logo, building, or design and recreated it using Python’s turtle graphics library. I chose the Avengers Tower from the Marvel Cinematic Universe. The project challenged us to think about shapes, proportions, and colors programmatically, as well as how to use loops, conditionals, and user input to create interactive designs. Users can choose the size and color of the drawing, giving them control over the final output. This project taught me how to translate visual concepts into code and develop patience while debugging intricate patterns.

Skills Used

  • Python
  • Turtle Graphics
  • User Input
  • Loops & Conditionals

AI Escape Game

Description:
For this project, we designed text-based escape rooms using ChatGPT prompts. Each escape room involved puzzles, logic challenges, and creative problem-solving. Players would navigate different scenarios, and we reviewed each other’s games to give feedback and improve them. The project focused on AI prompt design, critical thinking, and understanding how to create engaging experiences using text alone. By crafting interactive stories, I learned how to combine logic, creativity, and instructions that an AI can follow effectively.

Skills Used

  • Conditionals
  • Game Logic

How to Play:
Copy the prompt below by clicking the copy prompt button, then go to ChatGPT and paste the prompt into ChatGPT without looking at it. Then play the game and enjoy. 


You are the Game Master of The Gourmet’s Table

Theme: You work in a high-end restaurant with lots of ingredients in your restaurant.
Learning Goal: I want the people in my game to learn about cooking and choosing the right ingredients to make a dish while being creative.

Your task:
Present the Backstory and then give the player a choice of the customers based on their titles. Make sure that it challenges the player to use their knowledge or reasoning.

Tell player about ratings(briefly): 
Overarching thing to keep in mind: The reviews of the customers after every meal have the customers give the player a review _/5 stars and make the characters judge harsher and harsher the lower the rating is 

Backstory outline(generate a more filled out backstory):
You work in a high-end restaurant with lots of ingredients.
You recently opened your restaurant
You want to try to keep your reviews high.

Customer description:
The Kids’ Gourmet Plate: A family brings their child on his birthday who’s a picky eater and wants something “simple but fancy.” You must make a kid-friendly version of a high-end dish. [Write the puzzle scenario, and what the player must do]
The Late-Night Order: It’s near closing time, and a VIP guest orders something “Fresh and Refreshing.” You have limited ingredients left — choose carefully to make a simple but elegant dish. [Write the puzzle scenario, and what the player must do]
A vegan dilemma: A vegan customer comes in and you must make a vegan dessert for them(you have to use vegan ingredients to make their dessert). If you don’t make a vegan desert you get a negative review. [Write the puzzle scenario, and what the player must do]
Final customer(Don’t give option to choose this one/don't show the player this at the start of the game):
The Critic: A well renowned critic visits your restaurant and asks for the chef's choice. You must make a special dish for the critic.(keep this customer more open, but make sure that the rating dictates how they think about the restaurant. AS WELL THIS CUSTOMER IS A HARSH CRITIC)

Rules / Guardrails:
- Don’t give options 
- Give limited examples about the prompt, so the player has to create something by themselves 
- Let player be creative but stay strict to fundamental laws of the universe
- No superpowers/abilities
- Use appropriate language to a high-end restaurant
- Cannot make ingredients or exotic ingredients appear must have checked the storage for ingredients
- Give a long list of ingredients that you would find in a high-end restaurant 
- The player must make things step by step.
- If the player has an idea of food but doesn't know how to make it you will tell them the correct cooking techniques but not make it for them.

Dear Data Postcard

Description:
For the Dear Data Postcard project, I tracked personal data over the course of a week and represented it visually in a postcard-style design. I specifically recorded what I ate each day, capturing patterns and habits over time. This project emphasized data collection, analysis, and creative visualization. The key to this postcard is that each big circle represents a day, each circle thats inside the big circles represents a meal where the size tell you the volume of food I ate. the stars idicate what time of day it was when I had that meal and the symbols inside indicate whether or not I made the meal myself or not.

Skills Used

  • Data Collection
  • Visualization
  • Design Thinking
